Keeping in mind Reinhard Schulze (yesterday!):

It is also said that Awḥad al-Zamān became a Muslim when one day he attended the Caliph. After Awḥad al-Zamān had become a Muslim, he frequently used to renounce the Jews and curse them and insult them. What don't the biographers say?

  • performance of rituals (Jewish or Muslim, e.g. circumcision, ṣalāt, etc.)
  • statement of faith (beyond conversion itself)
  • parentage
  • explicit judgement about sincerity of conversion

3. More questions

What don't we know?

  • whether his wife converted
  • whether his daughters were married
  • Individual agency:
    • Were his adult daughters supposed to make their own decisions?
    • Or were they already married to Jewish husbands? (making conversion legally problematic)
    • Or did he want to preserver their membership in the Jewish community (including through future marriage to Jewish husbands)?
